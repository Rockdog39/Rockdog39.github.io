Meet Rory

My professional journey began in the motorcycle industry, where I have spent most of my working life helping operate and lead our family-owned motorcycle dealership. Over many years I progressed into the role of General Manager, gaining extensive experience in business operations, customer service, finance, team leadership, and strategic decision-making. Running a small business taught me resilience, problem-solving, and the importance of continuous improvement — principles that now strongly influence my approach to software development.

Alongside my professional career, motorcycle racing has played a defining role in my life. I competed internationally in the Australian Supersport, and British Superstock Championships, an experience that demanded discipline, technical understanding, risk management, and the ability to perform under pressure. Racing at this level reinforced a mindset of preparation, analysis, and incremental performance gains — qualities that translate directly into programming and systems development.

Today, my competitive focus has shifted toward endurance sports. Cross-country mountain bike racing and road cycling are my current passions, providing both physical challenge and mental clarity. Training and racing continue to develop my persistence, focus, and goal-oriented thinking, all of which support my transition into the technology industry.

I am currently completing a Bachelor of Information Technology with a focus on software development. My academic results reflect my commitment to this career change, with strong performance across programming and database subjects. I am particularly interested in building practical, efficient solutions and combining real-world business experience with modern software engineering practices.

My portfolio represents not only technical skills gained through study, but also a lifetime of problem-solving, leadership, and performance-driven thinking. I bring a unique perspective to software development — blending industry experience, competitive sport, and a passion for learning into a new career focused on technology and innovation.
